# Bonfire Night UK – Complete Guide (2026)

Bonfire Night, also known as Guy Fawkes Night, is one of the UK’s most widely celebrated annual events. Held on November 5th, it combines fireworks, bonfires, food, and community gatherings across the country.

This guide brings together everything you need to know – from history and traditions to modern celebrations, safety, and where to go.

---

## What is Bonfire Night?

Bonfire Night commemorates the failure of the Gunpowder Plot of 1605, when Guy Fawkes and his co-conspirators attempted to blow up the Houses of Parliament.

Today, it has evolved into a nationwide celebration featuring:
- Firework displays
- Bonfires
- Family-friendly events
- Food and drink stalls

---

## When is Bonfire Night 2026?

Bonfire Night is always on **November 5th**, but many organised events take place on the nearest weekend for convenience.

Expect most major displays to run between:
- Friday 30 October 2026
- Sunday 8 November 2026

---

## Types of Events

### 1. Large Public Displays
Organised by councils or event companies. These are:
- Ticketed or free
- Professionally run
- Safer and more impressive

### 2. Community Bonfires
Run by local groups or schools. Often include:
- Smaller fireworks
- Food stalls
- Family atmosphere

### 3. Private Fireworks
At home celebrations. These are increasingly regulated and should follow safety guidance.

---

## Firework Safety

Key rules:
- Buy from licensed sellers only
- Follow instructions carefully
- Keep a safe distance
- Supervise children at all times

Consider attending organised displays where possible.

---

## What to Expect at Events

Most events include:
- Fireworks display (15–30 minutes)
- Bonfire lighting
- Food (burgers, hot dogs, toffee apples)
- Drinks (mulled wine, hot chocolate)

Arrive early to avoid queues.

---

## Popular Locations

Some of the UK’s best-known displays include:
- London (multiple borough events)
- Lewes (famous traditional celebrations)
- Glasgow Green
- Cardiff events

Local listings are often the best option for families.

---

## Planning Your Night

To make the most of Bonfire Night:
- Check local listings early
- Book tickets in advance
- Dress warmly
- Plan transport (parking can be difficult)

Bonfire Night remains a key part of UK culture – combining history, community, and entertainment.

Whether you attend a major display or a local event, planning ahead ensures a safer and more enjoyable experience.
